Referit3D Benchmarks

Intro and Rules

We are interested in reporting the capacity of different approaches in identifying a 3D object based on referential language. To this end we use the same benchmark, and look at the same angles we investigated in the ReferIt3D paper.

Namely we consider:
  • How well a method works when the input language is Natural (Nr3D challenge) vs. Synthetic concerning only spatial-relations (Sr3D challenge)?
  • How well a method works when the number of same fine-grained instances present in a scene and referred in language are exactly two (Easy case, e.g., 2 office-chairs) vs. when the number of such distractors is larger (Hard case)
  • How well a method works when the linguistic reference makes use of View-Dependent expressions, e.g., "Facing the couch... on the right side", vs. View-Independent ones e.g., "the nightstand between the bed and the curtain".
